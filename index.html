About Me

I’m Dr. Haradhan Adhikary, a postdoctoral researcher and Assistant Professor at the University of Warsaw, specializing in experimental high-energy nuclear physics. My work focuses on exploring Quark-Gluon Plasma, critical phenomena in heavy-ion collisions, and neutrino oscillation physics. I’ve contributed extensively to the NA61/SHINE collaboration at CERN SPS in Geneva and am actively involved in neutrino physics research with the T2K and Super-Kamiokande collaborations in Japan. My research has been showcased at numerous international conferences and workshops. I’m passionate about discovery, collaboration, and pushing the boundaries of modern physics.
